The story behind Gogaku.

Why Gogaku exists

Most Japanese learning apps are built to keep you opening the app. Gogaku is built to help you actually learn Japanese — and then get out of your way.

The difference matters. Apps optimized for engagement push streaks, push notifications, and dopamine loops that make you feel like you're learning. Apps optimized for learning give you the tools, get out of the way, and let you do the work. Gogaku is the second kind.

No streaks. No leaderboards. No owls guilt-tripping you at 9pm. Just structured lessons, a real dictionary, smart flashcards, and a conversation partner — built to work together.

What makes it different

One app, four tools

Serious learners juggle three or four apps: a textbook app for grammar, a dictionary for lookups, a flashcard app for vocabulary, and a chat app for speaking practice. None of them talk to each other. Gogaku puts all four under one roof, and — more importantly — they share a brain. Words you learn in a lesson appear in your flashcard deck. Your AI chat partner knows what grammar you've covered. Your dictionary remembers what you've looked up.

Built progressively, not randomly

Every lesson stands on the last. The AI doesn't throw grammar at you that you haven't seen. Your flashcards focus on what you've actually encountered. It's the difference between a curriculum and a feed.

Honest pricing

The first thirteen lessons are free. The full dictionary is free. Unlimited flashcards are free. AI chat is free, with a daily limit. You only pay if you want intermediate and advanced content, or unlimited AI conversation. No free trial that auto-converts. No subscription you forget to cancel. There's even a one-time lifetime option, because subscription fatigue is real.
